Enabling QuickBooks users to connect directly to AU and UK banks

Product designer (Platform)
April 2017 - ongoing
Platforms
  • Web & mobile web (QuickBooks Online)
  • iOS & Android (QuickBooks Self-Employed)
Team

Platform: product designer, content designer, product manager, architect, 5 engineers

QuickBooks: 3-7 product managers, 5 engineers

Context

I designed an experience that enables small-business owners, self-employed users and accountants in Australia and the UK to directly connect and import financial data from their banks into QuickBooks Online (QBO) and QuickBooks Self-Employed (QBSE).

The experience has been released for several banks across these products and is still making progress towards an ideal state. For confidentiality reasons, various details are ommitted.

Problem

Broken bank connections prevent small-business owners and accountants from running their businesses

Most bank connection issues arise due to the limitations of screen-scraping, a secure but error-prone connection method.

User impact

When QuickBooks users get broken bank connections, they get gaps in data related to their transactions, expenses, and more. This means their accounting and bookkeeping is inaccurate. Ensuring their data comes in automatically, reliably and consistently helps users prosper.

Business impact

Bank connections break when banks update their website or change policies. These changes break scripts that periodically acquire data from banks, requiring engineering time to resolve. This gets expensive when engineers have to solve for tens of thousands of banks.

Bank impact

Screen-scraping makes many requests to bank servers, making it an expensive connection method for banks to maintain.

Solution

Partner with banks to provide a direct bank feed where the bank sends transactions directly to Intuit

instead of Intuit hitting bank servers and pulling user data periodically. This means users will no longer have bank errors or duplicate/missing data.

To get this direct bank feed, users need to authorize their banks to send the data to Intuit. For large banks in AU and UK, users apply with a Customer Authorisation Form (CAF).

Comparing connection methods

Screen-scraping

Pros
  • User gets data immediately
Cons
  • Connections prone to errors and duplicate transactions
  • Intuit causes heavy load on bank servers due to periodically requesting data

Direct bank feed

Pros
  • No connection errors
  • No duplicate transactions
  • User doesn't need to enter a username/password, less liability for Intuit and banks
  • Banks send transactions directly to Intuit
Cons
  • Application process is laborious
  • User doesn't get data immediately because application takes several days to approve

Understanding requirements

To design an experience taking users through this process, I worked with product managers, business development and government regulators to understand what AU and UK banks needed from users. Some requirements came from government rules while others were specific to the bank.

  • Users have to apply for the feed with a Customer Authorisation Form (CAF).
  • Each bank has its own form that requires varying amounts of information from users.
  • Only certain bank account types are eligible for direct feeds, depending on the bank.
  • Users need to print, hand-sign, and mail their forms to a specific mailing address to get their application processed.
  • Banks take several business days to process the form.
  • Product managers want to help new users get transactions in immediately. This means users need to connect via screen-scraping and also enroll in a direct feed.

Understanding users and their journey

This experience impacts small-business owners, self-employed users, and accountants. I also needed to consider differences for new users versus existing users.

Accountants
  • Aware of benefits of direct bank feeds
  • Have to contact clients to get banking information and signatures for form
Small-business owners
  • Unaware of benefits of direct bank feeds
  • Can have multiple partners they need info from
  • Usually has multiple business accounts
Self-employed users
  • Unaware of benefits of direct bank feeds
  • Mobile-based, need to consider how getting physical form will work
  • Often use personal bank accounts instead of business accounts

The end-to-end task of setting up a direct bank feed can't be completed in one continuous session. I worked with users, product managers, care agents and business development to map the process for each user group. Below is an example of one user journey.

Principles for an ideal experience

The complexities of this process and how it differs from the other connection methods revealed an opportunity to define how we should surface any connection option to users. Our platform team worked closely with the product groups to define principles that would drive our decisions.

  1. Help users get value from their data as soon as possible
  2. Minimize choice and confusion for users, we should know the best way to get their data quickly and reliably
  3. Ensure users continually get fresh and accurate data

Ideation

I consulted with QBO/QBSE designers, product managers, business development, backend architects, and engineers on the product teams and global teams to understand the timeline and constraints to produce a version 1 flow.

Testing

I prototyped the experience in InVision and tested several iterations with QuickBooks small-business owners. I surveyed a range of small-business owners with varying volumes of transactions. This helped me understand differences in comprehension and needs across various small-business types.

  • Unmoderated task completion with 32 AU users and 30 UK users
  • Measured for task success rate and flow comprehension
  • Product managers gathered qualititative verbal feedback from accountants

Insights

The biggest opportunity is influencing banks to simplify the process for users.

  • Usability of the flow performed well
  • Users didn't fully understand differences between a standard feed vs. direct bank feed
  • Major pain point was having to print and send the form and the time to activation

We iterated on the design based on these insights and released a version 1.

V1 Implementation

We released a happy path flow along with error-handling experiences, application rejection and resolution scenarios, and transaction reconciliation edge cases. V1 released on web first with a native iOS and Android release soon after. I provided interaction, visual and accessibility spec while our content designer provided text design.

Overall challenges

Solving edge cases related to existing users
Existing users had a hard time understanding the benefit of upgrading to direct bank feeds when the process was so laborious. Existing users already had data coming from screen-scraping. Migrating to a new connection method caused data issues that we had to guide users through resolving.

Reconciling integration and experience across different product tech stacks
Notification systems in QBO and QBSE are technologically different, making it difficult to coordinate messaging to users. Also, this direct bank feed feature integration had to fit in with both QBO and QBSE despite differences in their flows.

Aligning with other connection methods
Various AU and UK banks also use a connection method called WebAuth. We worked to consolidate and extend design patterns and technology to the WebAuth use case as well.

Solving bank specific use cases
Some banks had specific requests which we had to design and solve for. I worked closely with engineering to understand and ensure my design solutions for one bank could scale to another.

Impact

  • Increased QuickBooks’ direct bank feed market coverage in UK by 30% and in AU to 75%, surpassing competitors' coverage
  • Significant reduction in care calls received regarding screen-scraping connection issues as users migrate to direct bank feeds

Next steps

Given the constraints of what we could do in V1, there's ample opportunity to improve the experience.

  • How can we minimize manual work? What are the technical limitations of getting data and automating the process?
  • How can we position user tasks around their comprehension and mental models of bank connections?
  • How can we work with banks to simplify and update this laborious process while complying with regulations?

We're working through these enhancements and coordinating with product teams and financial institutions to reach an ideal state geared towards minimal work for the user. I’m currently designing and prototyping the optimal experience for each intermediate release.
