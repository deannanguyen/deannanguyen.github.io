Defining an ideal state for how Intuit users holistically manage bank connections

Workshop facilitator
November 2018

Goal

The Intuit Platform team is exploring how an Intuit customer using several products (TurboTax, QuickBooks, Mint) can manage all their bank connections. The goal of this workshop was to align stakeholders across the products on a vision for how this will work. For confidentiality reasons, various details are ommitted.

Role

I partnered with the design lead and product manager of this project to understand their objectives and tailored the session to meet their desired outcomes. My main role was to introduce, facilitate and time each module while providing guidance to keep the team focused.

Participants

15 stakeholders across Intuit Platform, TurboTax, QuickBooks and Mint. Participants included directors, product designers, product managers, backend architects, care leads and engineers. We used Mural to collaborate since participants were distributed across different offices.

Customer empathy

To prepare for the session, stakeholders participated in user interviews or follow-me-home sessions. All were asked to bring previous research and voice-of-customer feedback to share at the workshop.

Interviewing and journey mapping with QuickBooks users (small-business owner and tax accounting consultant)

Insights and affinity mapping

After talking to customers, we shared and documented our insights on user pain points and behaviors. We affinitized our learnings to narrow on high-level user pain points.

Defining the problem

We broke up into teams and each participant wrote a customer problem statement that they then shared with their team. The teams worked to narrow on one problem statement. Then we shared and discussed as a group to align on one focused customer problem statement to solve.

Defining the vision

After aligning on the problem, we thought about our vision for solving it. Again, each participant worked on their own vision statement and shared with their teams. Together we discussed and aligned on an ideal state.

Brainstorming capabilities

With a vision to work towards, everyone individually brainstormed ideas on how to get there. We affinitized the ideas we brainstormed into high-level themes and voted on which ideas would provide the biggest benefit to users. From the top-rated ideas, we prioritized those on a 2x2 axis comparing customer benefit and customer effort.

Outcomes

Stakeholders across the product teams aligned on a problem statement and a vision for solving the problem. Our platform team now has clear steps on how to go forward with designing and developing the feature and buy-in from our partners and stakeholders.
